Bilinear filtering

Bilinear filtering in AetherSX2 is an important feature to improve the visual quality of PlayStation 2 games run on Android devices. This feature works by smoothing image textures, reducing pixelation effects, and creating a smoother display.

  • Nearest-Neighbor (Point): Without smoothing, the image is sharp but rough.
  • Bilinear (Forced): Full smoothing, the image is smoother but can be slightly blurry.
  • Bilinear (PS2): Mimics the original PS2 smoothing, balanced between smooth and sharp.
  • Bilinear (Forced Excluding Sprite): Smoothing, except for 2D objects (sprites), maintaining their sharpness."

Mipmapping

Mipmapping is a feature in the AetherSX2 emulator to enhance the visual quality of PlayStation 2 games. It utilizes lower-resolution versions of image textures to minimize pixelation and flickering when objects are viewed at a distance.

Mipmapping Options in AetherSX2:

  • Automatic (Default): The emulator automatically selects the appropriate mipmapping type.
  • Off: Mipmapping is disabled, improving performance but reducing graphic smoothness.
  • Basic (Generate Mips): The emulator generates its own mipmaps.
  • Full (PS2 Mips): Uses mipmaps provided by the original PS2 game, offering the best graphics.

Mipmapping improves graphic fidelity, though it might impact performance. Choose the option that best suits your device's capabilities.

Trilinear Filtering

Trilinear Filtering enhances texture quality in AetherSX2, the PS2 emulator. This technique smooths textures in three dimensions, reducing visual artifacts and blurriness, especially on distant objects.

Trilinear Filtering Options:

  • Automatic (Default): The emulator automatically adjusts trilinear filtering usage, balancing quality and performance.
  • Disabled (Off): Trilinear filtering is deactivated, boosting performance at the cost of texture smoothness.
  • Enabled (PS2): Mimics the original PS2 trilinear filtering, providing authentic visual representation.
  • Enabled (Forced): Trilinear filtering is forcefully applied, yielding smoother images but demanding more device resources.

Trilinear filtering in AetherSX2 is a crucial feature for graphic enhancement, offering smoother and more realistic texture quality. With options like "Automatic," "Disabled," "PS2," or "Forced," users can tailor their visual experience as needed. Remember, device performance should be considered when enabling this feature.

Anisotropic Filtering

Anisotropic Filtering is a texture filtering technique that enhances image quality, especially when textures are viewed at oblique angles. It reduces blurriness and preserves texture detail, resulting in a sharper and more realistic display in PS2 games emulated on AetherSX2.

Anisotropic Filtering Options in AetherSX2:

  • Disabled (Default): This feature is deactivated, using basic texture filtering.
  • 2x, 4x, 8x, 16x: Anisotropic filtering levels, increasing texture sharpness. The higher the value, the better the quality, but also the greater the burden on the device.
